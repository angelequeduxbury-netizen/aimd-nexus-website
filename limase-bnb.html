Welcome

A guesthouse run the way this town was built — around people, not just passing trade.

Komani was laid out in 1853 with six streets radiating from a central hexagon, so that no corner of the settlement was ever far from the middle. Limase B&B works on the same principle: wherever you're headed next — the Stormberg, the Wild Coast, a farm auction, a family visit — you're never far from a comfortable, quiet base here in the middle of it all.

We keep the guesthouse small on purpose. A handful of rooms, kept spotless, a breakfast table where you'll actually talk to your hosts, and the kind of local knowledge you won't find on a booking site — where to fish, which pass is closed after rain, and which bakery opens first.

  • Family-run, with hosts on site every day
  • Central to town, the N6 and surrounding farm roads
  • Full cooked breakfast included with every stay

The Area

Komani, at the foot of the Stormberg

Set in the upper Komani River valley, the town is a natural stopping point for the Eastern Cape highlands — close enough to explore, quiet enough to rest.

In Town

The Hexagon

Komani's historic centre — the original hexagonal marketplace, now a National Monument, a short drive from the guesthouse.

Water & Wildlife

Bongolo Dam & nature reserve

Watersports, birdlife and hiking in the hills above town — a favourite half-day outing for guests.

On The Road

Gateway to the Stormberg

Well placed on the N6 for trips into the Stormberg and Winterberg mountains, and the wider Eastern Cape highlands.
